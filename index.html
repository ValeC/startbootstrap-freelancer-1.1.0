Place Spotter

Smartphone application
Master lecture project 2012-2013
University of Fribourg (Switzerland)

Objectif

During my Master studies, I followed a lecture of User-Centered Design (UCD) where I did a project in a group of three people. The theme was to build a memory aid application by following the stages of the UCD process.


Description

The application is a memory aid application to help users who want to trace their favourite places within or out of a city. An example of such places could be monuments, museums, shops, cafés, pubs, restaurants, parking place and sightseeing.


Method

We followed the UCD process iteratively in this way:
  • User research (surveys, personas, scenarios, storyboarding)
  • Prototyping (sketches, low- and high- fidelity prototypes)
  • Evaluation (video recording evaluation and discussion with the end users)


User research (surveys, personas, scenarios, storyboarding)
After surveyed 10 people on different group of people between 12 and 65 age through questionnaires, we could identify personas and scenarios (storyboards) for our application.

Prototyping (sketches, low- and high- fidelity prototypes)
After explored the possible users and scenarios, firstly we sketched some prototypes and then made it clickable in order to use them for the evaluation with the end users.
Sketches
Low-fidelity prototype

Evaluation (video recording evaluation and discussion with the end users)
We supported the philosophy to have a simple and intuitive user-friendly interface of our application, we considered whese aspects in order to evaluate it:
  • Number of clicks (taps) required to reach a goal
  • Number of error clicks (accuracy)
  • Time to complete assigned task (effciency)
  • Satisfaction score (i.e. if user liked the interface and if he/she is satisfed)
Users had 2 tasks to perform with two clickable prototypes. We performed the evaluation with 8 people. The result of the evaluation confirmed that the two prototypes are simple and intuitive because it takes little time, few clicks and few errors to accomplish the two tasks.

T - UCD

Web application
Master thesis 2013-2014
University of Fribourg (Switzerland)

Objectif

During my Master thesis, I studied the User-Centered Design process and then conceived an extensible tool for User-Centered Design beginners and practitioners by following the stages of this process.

Description

In the industry, User-Centered Design (UCD) has gained increasing acceptance thanks to the community awareness of its relevant benefits that improve the final prod- uct quality in terms of usability propriety. That leads to an increase of UCD beginners and currently it has not be found a tool that pedagogically accompanies them during the entire UCD process and its methods. This research has tried to overcome this lack by implementing an extensible web application, called “Tool for User-Centered De- sign“ (T-UCD) which provides a tutorial and a storage manager, in collaboration with the Swiss Federal O ce of Information Technology, Systems and Telecommunication (FOITT).


Method

In order to develop T-UCD, the UCD process has been applied throughout the three phases iteratively: user research, prototyping and evaluation.
  • User research (surveys, personas, scenarios, storyboarding)
  • Prototyping (sketches, low- and high- fidelity prototypes)
  • Evaluation (video recording evaluation and discussion with the end users)


User research (surveys, personas, scenarios, storyboarding)
At first, an online survey has been sent to .NET and JAVA developers employed in FOITT (16 people), in order to understand who the end-users of T-UCD are and to know about their opinion and awareness of UCD approach.
Thanks to the online survey, a UCD beginner persona and scenarios for T-UCD have been created.

Prototyping and Evalutation
Thanks to the “User research“ phase, it is possible to extract and set the requirements for T-UCD that cover the needs of UCD beginners:
  • a tutorial where UCD methods description, benefits, best practices and examples are described
  • a way of creating a new UCD project and see the existing projects
  • a structured form for each UCD methods as guidelines to perform them
  • a way to create, read, update and delete (CRUD) UCD methods
High-fidelity prototype
After decided the design of the homepage and the T-UCD, a clickable prototype has been conceived with Balsamiq in order to perform a formal usability evaluation for these pages.
With this prototype, it has been performed an evaluation of the user interface with some end users. The evaluation has been recorded with the accord of the user. Here below some videos of the evaluation.

Hydros Simulator

C++ software
Hydros innovation SA 2014-2015

Objectif

While working at Hydros (a design office specialised in energy efficiency located at the Innovation Park EPFL building C, www.hydros.ch) I gained valuable knowledge and experience in the conception and development of a new user interface. My main objective was to create a new user interface of a powerful tool of boats simulation based on an existing user interface considered not intuitive. In order to do that, I applied iteratively the process of UCD with the end users (naval engineers and architects). Then, I implemented it with C++, Qt and Ogre3D technologies.
