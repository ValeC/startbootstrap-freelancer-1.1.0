Place Spotter

smartphone application - Master lecture project 2012-2013

Objectif

During my Master studies, I did a project in a group of three people were the theme was to build a memory aid application by following the stages of the UCD process.


Description

The application is a memory aid application to help users who want to trace their favourite places within or out of a city and then track their way back. These so-called “users” are especially: people who travel a lot, people who has moved to a new geographic location, people who are not familiar with the structure of the city or people who have diffculties to remember different locations such as parking lots, shopping centre’s and so on. An example of such places could be monuments, museums, shops, cafés, pubs, restaurants and sightseeing.


Method

We followed the UCD process iteratively in this way:
  • 1. User research (surveys, personas, scenarios, storyboarding)
  • 2. Prototyping (sketches, low- and high- fidelity prototypes)
  • 3. Evaluation (video recording evaluation and discussion with the end users)


1. User research (surveys, personas, scenarios, storyboarding)
After surveyed 10 people on different group of people between 12 and 65 age through questionnaires, we could identify personas and scenarios (storyboards) for our application.

2. Prototyping (sketches, low- and high- fidelity prototypes)
After explored the possible users and scenarios, firstly we sketched some prototypes and then made it clickable in order to use them for the evaluation with the end users.

Low-fidelity prototype

3. Evaluation (video recording evaluation and discussion with the end users)
We supported the philosophy to have a simple and intuitive user-friendly interface of our application, we considered whese aspects in order to evaluate it:
  • Number of clicks (taps) required to reach a goal
  • Number of error clicks (accuracy)
  • Time to complete assigned task (effciency)
  • Satisfaction score (i.e. if user liked the interface and if he/she is satisfed)
Users had 2 tasks to perform with two clickable prototypes. We performed the evaluation with 8 people All this analysis con rm that the two prototypes are simple and intuitive because it takes little time, few clicks and few errors to accomplish the two tasks.
